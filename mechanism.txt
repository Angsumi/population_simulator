Ecosystem Simulation Mechanics (Incremental Realism Update - Level 8.3)

This document explains the biological rules driving the most up-to-date simulation (Level 8.3) in simple English. 

--- 1. Grass (Resources) ---
- Logistic Growth: Grass no longer spawns at a flat daily rate. Instead, it behaves organically by compounding at 5% of its current population per day (tick).
- Carrying Capacity: Growth mathematically halts smoothly as it approaches the `max_grass` cap (1,500) to prevent exponential explosion and lag.

--- 2. Deer (Herbivores) ---
- Foraging: Deer naturally steer towards the nearest visible grass patch and graze. 
- Gestation Delay: Eating fills an "energy pool." Once full (5 grass patches), the deer does not instantly split. Instead, it enters a gestation period, passing a 20% probability check each day to give birth. 
- Hiding Spots (Forests): Fleeing deer will seek camouflage in forests. Hiding spots no longer offer 100% magical invulnerability.
- Probabilistic Age Decay: Deer do not drop dead instantly on day 150. Instead, after day 150, they face a 2% chance of dying of old age, which increases by an additional 2% every subsequent day.

--- 3. Lions (Predators) ---
- Probabilistic Hunting: If a lion pounces on a deer, it only has a 70% chance to secure the catch. If it fails, the lion enters a "cooldown" phase, bouncing away and giving the deer time to escape.
- Diminished Vision: If a lion looks into a hiding spot/forest, its vision radius is reduced by 80%. If a lion wanders close enough (within that 20% remaining sight line), it can still spot and kill the hiding deer.
- Emergent Flocking (Death Spiral): When deer are scarce and hiding, lions often lose track of them at the forest edge, revert to a "wander" state, and accidentally lock into a circular flocking orbit (Milling) around the hiding spot due to their cohesion mechanics.
- Gestation & Decay: Similar to deer, fully fed lions have a 20% daily chance to reproduce. Old lions (250+ days) face the same escalating 2% probabilistic daily mortality chance.

--- 4. Visualizer Engine ---
- Decoupled Rendering: The simulation calculations run independently in a bare Python script. A background HTTP thread serializes the coordinate data to a live HTML5 Canvas frontend.
- Chronology: 1 Tick = 1 Day. The real-time line graph perfectly visualizes the Lotka-Volterra curves across 33 "Years" without ever interfering with the core biological speeds.

--- 5. Age-Structured Life Cycles (Level 8.2.3) ---
- Juveniles: Do not eat, hunt, or reproduce. Immune to starvation.
- Mature: The only phase capable of reproduction.
- Adults (Old): Continue to eat and hunt, but can no longer reproduce.

--- 6. Time & Starvation Realignment (Level 9 & 9.1) ---
- Time Scale: 1 Tick = 1 Day.
- Eating-Independent Breeding: Animals reproduce purely based on time (Deer drop 2 fawns annually, Lions drop 1 cub every 2 years) provided they survive to that age.
- Starvation Pressure: Animals must find food within 300 days or die. Because breeding is time-gated, they must consistently forage/hunt to survive until their breeding season.
